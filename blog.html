Encounters in the wild!

Scorpions:

  • The scorpion that calls Phantom Ranch in the Grand Canyon home is the Bark Scorpion.
  • They arethe most venomous in the United States.
  • The good news is that the bark scorpions are only lethal to their prey and luckily humans are not on that list!
  • They spend the day hiding and crawl out at night to hunt.They will eat crickets, moths, spiders and other insects.
  • Many compare their sting to an intense bee sting. Relief can be found with ice, aspirin and time.
  • When in the canyon, pay attention to where you are walking at night.Always wear your shoes or sandals.
  • Always have a light with you to see where you are going.
  • Never try to capture a scorpion and if you get stung, see your guide immediately.

Big horn sheep:

  • It is one of two species of wild sheep in North America.
  • It’s compact body is muscular with chocolate brown fur. Most weigh 160-250 lbs.
  • They have exceptional vision, hearing and smell that helps them detect and avoid predators.
  • If you encounter a big horn sheep, don’t agitate it.
  • The park service asks that you stay 100 feet away from big game animals.
  • Leave them enough room for escape if they feel threatened.
